1

INCOME

Your take-home pay for the selected period — the money that actually lands in your bank account after tax.

2

EXPENSES

All your bills added up. Clarity converts every bill to the current period automatically (e.g. a monthly bill becomes a fortnightly amount in fortnightly view).

3

BALANCE

What's left after all your bills. This is your spending money — or savings if you don't spend it. Aim for this to be positive.

4

SAVINGS %

What percentage of your income you keep after bills. Higher is better — aim for 20%+.

5

HEALTH score

A quick summary of how your budget looks right now:

✓ HEALTHY — saving 20%+ of income
⚠ WARNING — saving 5–19%, getting tight
✕ DEFICIT — spending more than you earn

1

Find the FY badge or TAX YEAR tab

On the Dashboard you'll see a purple pill near the top showing the current Financial Year — e.g. 📅 FY 2024–25. Tap it, or tap the 📅 TAX YEAR tab in the navigation bar.

2

Choose the Financial Year

At the top of the Tax Year screen there's a dropdown to pick which year to view. The current FY is selected automatically. In Australia the FY runs July to June (e.g. FY 2024–25 = Jul 2024 to Jun 2025).

💡 Clarity automatically uses the Australian FY calendar for AUD budgets — no setup needed.
3

See your annual totals

The summary cards show your total annual Income, Expenses, Balance and any items you've marked as Tax Deductible — all calculated for the selected FY.

4

Mark items as Tax Deductible

Scroll down to see all your bills listed. Each one has a ✓ Tax button. Tap it to mark that expense as tax deductible — it turns purple and is included in your deductible total. Tap again to un-mark it.

💡 Examples of deductible expenses: work-from-home internet, phone used for work, professional subscriptions. Ask your accountant if unsure.
5

Work From Home calculator

If you work from home, scroll to the WFH section. Enter your hours per week and weeks worked — Clarity calculates your ATO fixed-rate deduction ($0.67/hr) automatically.

6

Save a Snapshot

Click Save Snapshot to lock in your numbers for this FY — useful to compare year to year or share with your accountant before you change anything.

7

Print or Export for your accountant

Use Print Tax Summary to get a clean PDF page, or Export CSV to download a spreadsheet. Both include your income, expenses, deductibles and WFH claim.

📋 Give both documents to your accountant at tax time — they have everything needed to complete your Australian tax return.

HOW TO SAVE TO YOUR PREFERRED CLOUD — STEP-BY-STEP GUIDES

Download your backup ZIP above, then follow the guide for your cloud service below.

☁ iCloud Drive (iPhone / Mac)▶
1
Tap Download Backup.zip above
2
iPhone: open the Files app → On My iPhone → Downloads → find the ZIP
3
Tap and hold the ZIP → Move → select iCloud Drive → create a folder called Clarity Backup
4
Mac/Windows: open iCloud Drive in Finder or File Explorer → drag the ZIP into it
5
To restore: download the ZIP from iCloud Drive → tap Restore from Backup above
☁ OneDrive (Microsoft · 5 GB free)▶
1
Download your backup ZIP above
2
Go to onedrive.live.com or open the OneDrive app → sign in with your Microsoft account
3
Click Upload → Files → select the backup ZIP → it uploads in seconds
4
To restore: download from OneDrive → tap Restore from Backup above
☁ Dropbox (2 GB free)▶
1
Download your backup ZIP above
2
Open the Dropbox app or go to dropbox.com → sign in
3
Tap + → Upload files → select the ZIP · 2 GB free covers years of Clarity backups
4
To restore: download from Dropbox → tap Restore from Backup above
📧 Email to Yourself (simplest option)▶
1
Download your backup ZIP above
2
Open Gmail, Outlook, or your email app → compose a new email to yourself
3
Attach the ZIP → subject it "Clarity Backup — [today's date]" → send
4
The email lives in your inbox forever — search "Clarity Backup" anytime to find it
5
To restore: open the email, download the ZIP → tap Restore from Backup above
ℹ GOOGLE DRIVE SETUP NOTE (for Deep)
Google Drive auto-sync requires a one-time OAuth Client ID. Steps:
1. Go to console.cloud.google.com → New project → Enable Google Drive API
2. Credentials → Create OAuth 2.0 Client ID → Web application → add your domain to authorised origins
3. Replace YOUR_GOOGLE_CLIENT_ID in the source code with the Client ID
Until then, the Download Backup ZIP method is just as secure — no setup needed.
